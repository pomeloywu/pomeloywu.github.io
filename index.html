Candy Bar Ads Campaign Sales Lift Analysis

  • Report

Abstract: The effectiveness of the death penalty in deterring violent crime is a long-standing debate in the United States. Some believe that the death penalty acts as a deterrent and lowers violent crime rates, while others argue that the death penalty may actually contribute to an increase in violent crime by normalizing violence as a means of punishment. Policymakers need accurate information to make informed decisions about whether to retain or abolish the death penalty. Our results show that the presence of the death penalty leads to increases in the violent crime rate, suggesting that the death penalty may not have a significant deterrent effect on violent crime. However, this result is not robust across states. In our case studies of Connecticut, Illinois, and Marland, we only found a consistent result in Connecticut. This further suggests that policymakers should also consider the specific characteristics of their state when deciding whether to retain or abolish the death penalty. In addition, economic conditions may play a larger role in the occurrence of violent crime than the presence or absence of the death penalty.

Breast Cancer Mass Lesion Analysis

  • GitHub Repo

Breast cancer is the most commonly diagnosed cancer in women in the United States, it is highly desired to detect and introduce intervention in the early stage to prevent further complications. Therefore, there has been a high demand in utilizing machine learning on mammography data to assist diagnosis. This analysis aims to determine the effects that a breast lesion’s physical attributes have on a lesion’s severity.

My final findings suggest that there are significant relationships between the odds of developing a malignant breast lesion and BI-RADS score, its mass shape and mass margin, as well as patient age, which has the strongest association with malignancy.
