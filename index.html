UAS Semantic Segmentation for Safe Landing

  • Report

Abstract: The ethics and safety of UAVs have been called into question for some time, but with so much environmental and social upside, developing safe flying and landing is of the utmost importance. In order to enhance drone safety, our research is concerned with applying state of the art deep learning models to solve semantic segmentation with drone cameras. To evaluate the performance of our models we used several metrics common in semantic segmentation problems including the jaccard index. Our models were able to achieve 0.8 multi class weighted jaccard index, 0.4 unweighted multi class jaccard index, and .85 binary unweighted jaccard index which is on par with other semantic segmentation problems in other domains. However, there remain some shortcomings of our model for specific classes that we hope to address in upcoming research.

The Effectiveness and Implications of Death Penalty

  • Report

Abstract: The effectiveness of the death penalty in deterring violent crime is a long-standing debate in the United States. Some believe that the death penalty acts as a deterrent and lowers violent crime rates, while others argue that the death penalty may actually contribute to an increase in violent crime by normalizing violence as a means of punishment. Policymakers need accurate information to make informed decisions about whether to retain or abolish the death penalty.

Our results show that the presence of the death penalty leads to increases in the violent crime rate, suggesting that the death penalty may not have a significant deterrent effect on violent crime. However, this result is not robust across states. In our case studies of Connecticut, Illinois, and Marland, we only found a consistent result in Connecticut. This further suggests that policymakers should also consider the specific characteristics of their state when deciding whether to retain or abolish the death penalty. In addition, economic conditions may play a larger role in the occurrence of violent crime than the presence or absence of the death penalty.

Opioid Intervention Policy Analysis

  • Report

Over the past two decades, the United States has grappled with a significant surge in prescription opioid abuse, resulting in a stark escalation in opioid addiction and fatalities. Compounding this issue, individuals ensnared by addiction often resort to illicit markets, exacerbating the already high rates of drug overdoses. While policy interventions have the potential to reduce the volume of prescription opioids, they may inadvertently drive the consumption of more potent illegal opioids, amplifying the risk of fatality. Given this complex scenario, an examination of the impact of policy interventions on drug consumption and mortality rates holds critical importance for policymakers, aiding in the formulation of effective strategies to combat drug overdoses in the future.

Unveiling Literary Insights: A Journey Through Book Genre Classification

  • GitHub Repo
  • Report

This project focuses on using various generative and discriminative models to predict book genres based on their synopses. The dataset sourced from Kaggle initially contained ten different classes, but due to the limited data samples in some genres, the analysis was streamlined to focus on six primary genres. By selecting 500 random samples from each genre, we aimed to create a balanced dataset for effective model training and analysis. Our results compared different model implementations and performance, providing valuable insights into the world of literary classification.
